Earthquakes

Earthquakes occur every day, especially along plate boundaries. Some are too small to be noticed, while others cause widespread destruction. This map shows all of the earthquakes that occurred over a week in 2006. The most recent earthquakes are the most opaque, fading away over time.

What do the colors mean?

Each earthquake is represented by a circle. The color indicates the Richter scale value: yellow dots have a magnitude of two or less; orange dots have a magnitude between two and five; and red dots have a magnitude greater than five.
