Ocean Draining

Oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth’s surface, and far below the waves lies a vast “landscape” of mountains, plains and canyons. Ships have mapped only a tiny fraction of the sea floor, but we can use that information to create a model of what the planet would look like if the oceans were drained.

CREDIT: Paul Butler, Facebook

What do the colors mean?

Deeper regions—trenches and canyons—are dark blue. Lighter blues and greens show higher areas, such as undersea plains and mountain ranges.
