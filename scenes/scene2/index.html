Japanese Tsunami

On March 11, 2011, a 9.0 magnitude earthquake occurred 81 miles of the east coast of Japan, triggering a massive tsunami. Forecast models use data from buoys in the ocean to predict wave arrival times, locations, and heights, helping to protect lives and property. Here, the Japanese tsunami prediction appears first, followed by the actual maximum height of the waves and tsunami arrival time.

CREDIT: Chris Elvidge, National Geophysical Data Center, NOAA / Robert Simmon, NASA Earth Observatory

What do the colors mean?

The yellow dots are buoys that report sea levels to the Tsunami Warning Centers. The information they provide gets fed into models and makes the forecasts more accurate.
